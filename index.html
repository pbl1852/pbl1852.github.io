01 · Introduction

Why This Analysis Matters

StreamKUS is a new media company preparing to release its first original content on YouTube and Netflix. Since audience preferences change quickly, relying only on intuition can be risky. Therefore, this project uses a data-driven approach to identify what kinds of genres, themes, and story elements appeal to viewers.

For this report, our team analyzed eight popular contents across four genre categories: romance, thriller/crime, human and social documentary drama, and comedy. The selected contents were Single’s Inferno and When Life Gives You Tangerines for romance, Squid Game and Parasyte for thriller, There Are No Bad Dogs in the World and The Trauma Code for slice of life, and SNL Korea and Shortbox for comedy. By comparing viewer reactions to these works, we aimed to discover patterns that can guide StreamKUS’s future content planning.

Genres Covered

We picked 4 different genres: romance, slice of life, thriller, and comedy, and picked 2 shows from each genre to analyze. The reason we divided our analysis targets into genres was because we would get a better understanding of what appeals more to what audience, and for what reason. We picked these 4 genres because they are historically among the most popular genres, and we thought that this would help us get a better idea for planning content.

Dramas: We used the most watched Netflix shows from 2024-25 from each genre: 1 romance, 1 slice of life, and 3 thriller. For Korean reviews, we used Watchapedia (pedia.watcha.com), and for English reviews, we used Rotten Tomatoes (rottentomatoes.com). For each show, we used Codex to collect all user reviews available without post filtering, and piled them into a CSV file.

Reality: We used 1 romance, 1 slice of life, and 2 comedy reality shows that gained traction in recent years, aired on Netflix, TV, YouTube, and Coupang Play respectively. We collected YouTube comments and posts on social media sites Nate (nate.com) and DCInside (dcinside.com), and piled them into a CSV file using Codex for analysis.

For dramas, we used audience reviews, not critic reviews, as we thought that the overall reviews of a wider audience would be more fit for our needs than the more professional critic reviews. The same thought process went into collecting reality show reviews, as we thought YouTube comments as well as social media posts would be able to represent a wider audience, therefore leading to a better understanding of what the wider audience yearns.

03 · Text Mining Methods

Analytical Techniques Used

Method 01

Sentiment Analysis

We used Colab to analyze the sentiment of the collected text data.

Method 02

AI-Assisted Review Analysis

Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite Preview was used to summarize audience reactions, key themes, and repeated review patterns.

Method 03

Genre-Based Strategy

Positive and negative points were compared by genre and show to decide what StreamKUS should do and avoid.

We collected the sentiment analysis of the collected text using Colab, and with Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite Preview we used the following prompt:

"당신은 영화/TV 평론가이자 데이터 분석가입니다. 전체 리뷰 중 {sample_size}개를 무작위로 추출한 데이터를 바탕으로 종합적인 리뷰 분석을 작성해 주세요. 요구사항: **주의사항: 모든 분석 결과와 응답값은 반드시 '한국어(Korean)'로 작성해 주세요.** 이 작품에 대한 사람들의 반응과 점수 분석 근거를 한국어로 명확하게 작성하세요. 긍정적 피드백, 부정적 피드백, 반복되는 주제를 배열 형태로 매우 상세하게 요약하세요. **중요**: 각 항목은 반드시 '**주제:** 설명' 형식으로 작성하세요. (예시: '**세계관의 확장:** 단순히 게임을 반복하는 것이 아니라...'). 샘플링된 리뷰들에서 가장 많이 언급된 핵심 키워드 상위 10개를 추출하고, 해당 키워드가 샘플 리뷰 내에서 대략 몇 번 언급되었는지 빈도수를 추정하여 포함하세요. 결론을 한국어로 작성하세요."

The result printed the overall sentiment score out of 10, the frequency of each number, main positive and negative points, and the most frequently mentioned topics and keywords.

Because our end goal is proposing content for our streaming platform, we analyzed the main positive and negative points for each genre/show and used them to pinpoint what we need to do and what to avoid when making a show for each genre.

06 · Content Proposals

Strategic Recommendations

1

Romance Reality Drama

StreamKUS should create a romance content that emphasizes emotional immersion, sincere relationships, and visible chemistry between characters.

2

Survival Thriller Series

Thriller content should include high tension, survival situations, and unpredictable conflicts while avoiding repetitive story structures.

3

Human Social Drama

Human and social drama should focus on empathy, realistic problems, and characters that reflect real-life emotions.

4

Short-Form Comedy Series

Comedy content should use everyday situations, fast pacing, and relatable humor that can spread easily on YouTube and social platforms.

07 · Limitations

Research Constraints

⚠️
Limited data scope

The analysis was based on selected contents and comments, so the findings may not represent all viewers.

⚠️
Platform bias

Viewer reactions may differ depending on the platform where comments were collected.

⚠️
Interpretation limits

Some reactions may include sarcasm or context-dependent meanings that are difficult to capture through simple keyword analysis.

08 · Conclusion

Key Takeaways

This project shows that viewer reactions can provide useful guidance for content planning. By comparing audience responses across romance, thriller/crime, human and social drama, and comedy, StreamKUS can design future content based on actual viewer interests rather than intuition alone.
